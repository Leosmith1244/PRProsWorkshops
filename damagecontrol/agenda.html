Workshop Agenda

Damage Control Dungeon: Survive a PR Apocalypse

Detailed Workshop Timeline

Equipping members with persuasive communication skills to handle PR crises using Toulmin’s Model of Argumentation.

Date: December 2025 | Total Duration: 90 minutes

0:00 - 0:10 (10 mins)

Guest and Member Introductions

Objective: Welcome attendees and foster connection.

  • Toastmaster of the Day welcomes everyone and introduces the workshop theme, “Grace Under Fire.”
  • Members and guests share brief introductions (name, role, one-sentence reaction to the theme).
  • Timekeeper ensures each introduction is 15-20 seconds.

Notes for Success:

  • Keep energy high; use a fun icebreaker question.

0:10 - 0:13 (3 mins)

Introduction to Toulmin’s Model

Objective: Explain Toulmin’s Model to frame the speeches.

  • Speaker (or Toastmaster) introduces Toulmin’s Model: Claim, Evidence, Warrant, Backing, Qualifier, Rebuttal.
  • Use a simple example to illustrate the model.
  • Highlight relevance to PR crises and “Grace Under Fire.”

Notes for Success:

  • Use a visual aid (e.g., slide or handout); keep explanation concise and engaging.

0:13 - 0:23 (10 mins)

Speech 1: Oops to Ovation

Objective: Show how to spin a scandal into a positive outcome.

Speaker: Expert 1

  • Speech (6 minutes): Speaker presents a fake Toastmasters scandal (plagiarism at a conference) using Toulmin’s Model, emphasizing evidence (e.g., audit, ethics training).
  • Activity (4 minutes): Teams simulate a scandal (e.g., “misused funds”). Each crafts a 30-second response using Toulmin’s Model, focusing on evidence. Peers vote on the best spin.

Notes for Success:

  • Timekeeper ensures speech stays within 6 minutes; activity is fast-paced.

0:23 - 0:24 (1 min)

Transition

Toastmaster recaps speech 1’s use of evidence, previews speech 2’s focus on qualifiers for non-apologies.

0:24 - 0:34 (10 mins)

Speech 2: The Non-Apology Apology

Objective: Teach crafting a regretful yet fault-free apology.

Speaker: Expert 2

  • Speech (6 minutes): Speaker role-plays a politician addressing a “misinterpreted” comment, using Toulmin’s Model with strong qualifiers.
  • Activity (4 minutes): Pairs role-play a politician and critic. One delivers a 30-second non-apology; the other critiques. Switch roles. Discuss qualifier effectiveness.

Notes for Success:

  • Ensure activity encourages creativity; monitor time closely.

0:34 - 0:35 (1 min)

Transition

Toastmaster highlights qualifiers in speech 2, previews speech 3’s focus on warrants for pivoting attention.

0:35 - 0:45 (10 mins)

Speech 3: The Distraction Gambit

Objective: Demonstrate pivoting attention from a scandal to a positive narrative.

Speaker: Expert 3

  • Speech (6 minutes): Speaker addresses a company’s product defect crisis, using Toulmin’s Model to justify a distraction campaign (e.g., sustainability initiative).
  • Activity (4 minutes): Small groups pitch a 30-second distraction for a fake scandal. Peers evaluate warrant strength.

Notes for Success:

  • Keep activity engaging; encourage bold distraction ideas.

0:45 - 0:46 (1 min)

Transition

Toastmaster recaps warrants in speech 3, previews speech 4’s competitive rebuttals.

0:46 - 0:56 (10 mins)

Speech 4: PR Thunderdome

Objective: Show how to win a competitive PR battle.

Speaker: Expert 4

  • Speech (6 minutes): Speaker presents a district’s response to an “elitist” comment scandal, using Toulmin’s Model with strong rebuttals.
  • Activity (4 minutes): Two teams pitch 1-minute reputation-saving plans for the same scandal. Peers (as judges) vote on the stronger rebuttal and evidence.

Notes for Success:

  • Foster a competitive vibe; ensure judges focus on Toulmin’s components.

0:56 - 0:57 (1 min)

Transition

Toastmaster summarizes the workshop’s use of Toulmin’s Model, sets up the debrief.

0:57 - 1:07 (10 mins)

Debrief

Objective: Reflect on lessons learned and Toulmin’s Model application.

  • Toastmaster facilitates a discussion on how Toulmin’s Model helped structure responses.
  • Invite 2-3 attendees to share insights.
  • Speaker summarizes key takeaway.

Notes for Success:

  • Encourage participation; timekeeper ensures discussion stays focused.

1:07 - 1:22 (15 mins)

Club Business

Objective: Address club updates and planning.

  • President or Vice President leads announcements (upcoming events, membership, dues, contests).
  • Assign roles for next meeting.
  • Open floor for brief member input.

Notes for Success:

  • Keep organized; reserve 2 minutes for closing remarks and adjournment.
